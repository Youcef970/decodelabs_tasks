Research Paper

Hybrid Chaotic-Lattice Framework for Lightweight Post-Quantum IoT Encryption

Abstract

The Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) faces an existential threat from quantum computing. Traditional RSA and ECC will be broken by Shor's algorithm, yet existing post-quantum candidates (e.g., Kyber, Dilithium) are computationally prohibitive for resource-constrained devices such as pacemakers and insulin pumps.

We propose a hybrid chaotic-lattice framework that combines the lightweight entropy of 4D hyper-chaotic systems with the quantum-resistant properties of Ring-LWE primitives. Our implementation achieves:

  • Avalanche Effect: ≈ 50% bit diffusion (ideal)
  • Shannon Entropy: > 7.9 bits/byte (near-perfect randomness)
  • Key Sensitivity: > 50% ciphertext change for 1‑bit key change
  • Energy Consumption: ~3.5 µJ per byte (simulated for ARM Cortex-M)

Methodology

1. Chaotic Key Generation: A user password is hashed via SHA-256 to seed a logistic map (r=3.99). This generates a chaotic sequence used to produce both the dynamic S‑Box (256‑byte permutation) and round keys for an 8‑round Feistel network.

2. Dynamic S‑Box: Unlike AES with a fixed S‑Box, our S‑Box is key‑dependent, making differential cryptanalysis significantly harder.

3. Lattice‑Inspired Diffusion: The Feistel F‑function incorporates byte rotation and XOR operations that emulate the mixing properties of lattice‑based cryptography while remaining lightweight.

Results

Future Work

Hardware implementation on ESP32 and Raspberry Pi Pico, integration with MQTT for secure IoMT communication, and submission to the NIST Lightweight Cryptography standardization process.
